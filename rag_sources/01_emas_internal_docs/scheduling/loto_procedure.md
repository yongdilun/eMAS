# LOTO Procedure Standard

## 1. Overview
Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) is a safety procedure used to ensure that dangerous machines are properly shut off and not able to be started up again prior to the completion of maintenance or repair work.

## 2. Energy Isolation
All energy sources must be identified and isolated. This includes electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical, thermal, and other energy sources.

### 2.1 Electrical Isolation
Ensure the main circuit breaker is in the OFF position and a lockout device is applied with a padlock.

### 2.2 Mechanical Isolation
Relieve any stored mechanical energy, such as springs under tension or elevated machine parts that could fall.

## 3. Lockout Steps
1. Notify all affected employees.
2. Shut down the equipment using normal procedures.
3. Isolate the equipment from energy sources.
4. Apply lockout/tagout devices.
5. Release or restrain any stored energy.
6. Verify isolation by attempting to start the machine.

## 4. Safety Warning
Failure to follow LOTO procedures can result in serious injury or death. Only authorized employees are permitted to perform lockout procedures.
