## 5.10 User Survey and Usability Feedback Results

A small task-based usability survey was conducted to collect feedback on the usability and usefulness of the eMAS system. The survey involved 10 participants and used a five-point Likert scale, where 1 represents Strongly Disagree, 2 represents Disagree, 3 represents Neutral, 4 represents Agree, and 5 represents Strongly Agree. The purpose of the survey was to evaluate whether users could understand and use the main functions of the system, including dashboard monitoring, job and inventory access, scheduling, shortage resolution, AI-assisted interaction, and approval-based safety control.

Since eMAS was evaluated as a prototype and was not deployed in a live factory environment, the survey was used as an early usability evaluation rather than a large-scale industrial user study. The participants were asked to review the system and rate statements related to navigation, clarity, usefulness, automation support, decision support, and safety. The survey results are useful because they provide user-facing evidence in addition to the automated testing evidence reported in the previous sections.

Table 5.8 shows the survey statements and average scores.

| No. | Survey Statement | Mean Score |
|---:|---|---:|
| 1 | The system interface is easy to navigate. | 5.00 |
| 2 | The dashboard helps me understand the factory status. | 4.70 |
| 3 | Job and inventory information can be found easily. | 4.40 |
| 4 | The scheduling result is understandable. | 4.60 |
| 5 | The shortage-resolution result clearly explains the material problem. | 4.90 |
| 6 | The AI assistant understands factory-related questions. | 4.80 |
| 7 | The AI assistant provides useful information for decision-making. | 4.50 |
| 8 | The approval workflow makes data-changing actions feel safer. | 4.50 |
| 9 | The system can reduce manual checking work compared with doing the task manually. | 4.70 |
| 10 | Overall, the system is useful for factory-management support. | 5.00 |

The overall mean score across all survey items was 4.71 out of 5.00. This indicates that the participants generally agreed or strongly agreed that eMAS is usable and useful for factory-management support. Out of 100 total ratings collected across all survey questions, 99 ratings were either Agree or Strongly Agree, giving an overall positive response rate of 99%.

The strongest results were recorded for interface navigation and overall usefulness, both with a mean score of 5.00. This suggests that participants found the system easy to use and considered it useful for factory-management support. The shortage-resolution result also received a high mean score of 4.90, showing that participants generally understood the material problem explanations provided by the system. The AI assistant also received a strong score of 4.80 for understanding factory-related questions, which supports the system objective of providing AI-assisted interaction.

The lowest mean score was 4.40 for finding job and inventory information easily. Although this is still a positive result, it suggests that job and inventory navigation can be improved further. For example, future improvements may include clearer search filters, stronger cross-linking between job and inventory pages, and more direct links from shortage rows to related material or job records.

Overall, the survey results support the automated evaluation findings. The participants responded positively to the usability, usefulness, AI assistance, shortage explanation, and approval safety of eMAS. However, the survey was limited to a small prototype evaluation and did not involve deployment in an actual factory environment. Therefore, future work should conduct a larger usability study with real factory users after the system is connected to live operational data.
